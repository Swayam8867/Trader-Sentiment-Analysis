# Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment Analysis

## Overview
This project investigates how market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) influences trader performance and decision-making. By combining trading activity with sentiment data, the analysis identifies patterns in profitability, risk exposure, and behavioral tendencies.

---

## Approach
- Cleaned and standardized trade and sentiment datasets  
- Aligned data on a daily level for consistent comparison  
- Derived key metrics such as PnL, trade size, and trading frequency  
- Segmented traders based on behavior (size and frequency)  
- Compared performance across different sentiment conditions  

---

## Key Findings

- **Market sentiment matters:** Trader profitability is higher during Greed periods than Fear periods  
- **Risk drives returns:** Larger trade sizes lead to higher profits but increase exposure to losses  
- **Behavioral edge:** Infrequent traders outperform frequent traders, indicating overtrading reduces efficiency  
- **Amplified outcomes:** High-risk traders benefit more in positive sentiment but are more vulnerable in negative sentiment  

---

## Practical Implications

- Adjust position sizing based on market sentiment  
- Reduce exposure during Fear-driven markets  
- Focus on selective, high-quality trades instead of frequent execution  
- Manage risk more aggressively when using high leverage or large trade sizes
